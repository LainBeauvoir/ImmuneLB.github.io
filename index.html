IMMUNE

A Journey Into the Mysterious System That Keeps You Alive

Immune is a structured educational platform dedicated to explaining the immune system through clear organization and conceptual coherence. By presenting immunology as an interconnected system rather than a list of facts, it supports understanding across cells, pathogens, and biological processes while offering concise, scientifically accurate explanations for students, educators, and curious readers. Inspired by the richly illustrated and accessible approach of Philipp Dettmer, creator of the science channel Kurzgesagt—In a Nutshell, Immune aims to transform the way you think about your body through clear, engaging exploration of the immune system.

PURPOSE

The purpose of Immune is to make immunology more navigable without reducing its complexity. By separating concepts into clear categories and concise explanations, the platform aims to support students, educators, and curious readers seeking a reliable point of reference alongside textbooks and academic sources.

CATALOGUES

The Catalogue serves as the structural core of Immune. It organizes immunological knowledge into three primary domains: body cells, pathogens, and biological processes. Each entry focuses on function, interaction, and context, emphasizing how individual components contribute to the immune system as a whole.

GLOSSARY

Immunology relies heavily on precise terminology. The Glossary provides clear, academically grounded definitions of key terms, abbreviations, and concepts used throughout the site. Definitions prioritize accuracy and context rather than simplification, making them suitable for both introductory learning and revision.
